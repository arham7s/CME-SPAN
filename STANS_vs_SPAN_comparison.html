STANS

OCC — Options Clearing Corp

System for Theoretical Analysis and Numerical Simulations · Est. 2006

SPAN

CME Group

Standard Portfolio Analysis of Risk · Est. 1988

Dimension STANS (OCC) SPAN (CME)
Core method Large-scale Monte Carlo simulation (10,000+ scenarios per risk factor) Grid-based scenario simulation (16 predefined risk scenarios per product)
Risk measure 99% Expected Shortfall (ES) over a 2-day horizon Value at Risk (VaR) — worst likely loss over 1 trading day
Approach type Multivariate — models all assets jointly via copula functions Primarily univariate — evaluates each product group separately
Correlation modelling Explicit copula captures tail dependence across all ~8,000+ assets Inter-commodity spreads provide limited offsets within product groups
Tail risk capture Strong — ES accounts for losses beyond the VaR threshold; fat-tailed innovations modelled Weaker — VaR can underestimate extreme loss events beyond the worst scenario
Products covered Equity/index options, futures, FLEX options, volatility futures, stock loans Futures, options on futures, physicals, equities, OTC swaps (via SPAN 2)
Margin horizon 2-day liquidation horizon 1-day liquidation horizon
Volatility treatment Implied vol surface modelled with skew across tenors and deltas; volatility clustering allowed Fixed volatility scan ranges defined by exchange; less dynamic
Adoption Proprietary to OCC — not licensed externally Global standard — used by 50+ exchanges and clearinghouses worldwide
Transparency Publicly documented via SEC filings; detailed model whitepapers available Well-documented by CME; parameters (scan ranges, spreads) published daily
Computational intensity Very high — full Monte Carlo across 40,000+ inputs daily Lower — scenario grid is fast and replicable; industry-friendly
Evolution Ongoing — recent enhancements for short-dated options, vol surface extensions SPAN 2 launched: adds HVaR, stress VaR, liquidity/concentration charges, broader product scope

STANS strength

More accurate tail-risk capture and cross-asset correlation via full simulation. Better suited for complex options portfolios.

SPAN strength

Speed, simplicity, and global interoperability. Easily replicated by clearing members for pre-trade margin checks.
